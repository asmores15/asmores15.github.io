Hello! I've been working on finding a job for the past two months and dealing with life stuff, so I haven't done much with the 8080 project.
I also kinda hit a wall with it. Turns out it's pretty complicated to recreate! Or at least it is for me. I don't have a ton of experience with 
designing CPU architecture unfortunately, so I'm a little stuck. 

I've decided to put that on the back burner for now and come back to it later. Maybe I'll make my own MIPS CPU first, since I'm familiar with the MIPS architecture
and it would be easier to start with that.

Anyway, I have a new project in mind! Not sure what to call it. Water stopper? Stream stopper? Faucet pauser? I have no idea. 

What it's supposed to do is have a button somewhere on the sink, maybe near the faucet handles, and when it's pressed, it stops the water for about 5 seconds or so.

When I'm doing dishes, sometimes I need to scrub and not have the dish in the running stream of water. But, while I'm scrubbing, the water keeps running, which is just a waste.
But if I wanted to turn off the water, I'd have to stop, turn the handles off, scrub, and then turn the handles back on to my desired pressure and temperature. It just gets inconvenient
when all I could do is just let it run. However, like I said, that would be wasteful! I also live in a desert so the less water waste the better.

So, I figured that it would be nice to have a device of some sort that can turn off the stream of water without having to mess with the handles. Then, I can scrub for 5-10 seconds and then
have the water come back on automatically so I can rinse the dish.

I hope that all makes sense! I've been looking into it a little and I think it can be done! I'll talk about it more in my next post.

Thanks for reading!
